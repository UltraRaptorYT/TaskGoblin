# TaskGoblin Motion Identity

## Style prompt

Technical, cinematic, and playful. The film should feel like a high-stakes product launch interrupted by a clever goblin who turns messy team chatter into structured, accountable work. Use crisp interface choreography, strong typographic scale, scan-line and grid details, and small moments of mischievous character animation.

## Format

- 1920 × 1080, 16:9
- 60 seconds
- 30 fps
- Seven concise scenes with continuous visual momentum

## Color system

- Cinematic forest: `#07140E`
- Panel green: `#173D2B`
- Goblin lime: `#DFFF64`
- Warm parchment: `#F4F1E8`
- Signal mint: `#63D9B1`
- Warning ember: `#FFB454`

Lime is the primary accent. Mint and ember are functional signals, not competing brand colors.

## Typography

- Geist, 800–900: headlines, product statements, scene-defining numbers
- Geist Mono, 500–700: metadata, timestamps, scan labels, evidence and status
- Headlines are compact and bold; supporting copy is short enough to read at a glance.

## Motion language

- Confident push transitions and cinematic blur crossfades
- One zoom-through transition for the TaskGoblin product reveal
- Interface elements assemble in meaningful order: source → inference → task → owner → reminder
- The mascot gets restrained elastic motion and tiny mischievous tilts
- No ambient infinite loops; all motion is deterministic and tied to the timeline

## Visual motifs

- Perspective grids and faint routing lines
- Chat bubbles becoming task cards
- Scan bars, source citations, confidence values and status chips
- Oversized ghost typography used for depth
- Soft lime glows around decisive actions

## Avoid

- Generic purple-and-blue AI gradients
- Stock photography or generic office imagery
- Dense dashboards shown all at once
- Tiny unreadable UI text
- Excessive glitch, neon, or gamer aesthetics
- Violent or threatening imagery; the goblin is cheeky, not cruel
